The pipeline

Seven stages, one source of truth

Every stage feeds directly on the output of the previous one — the same data flows end to end, with no copy-paste and no parallel spreadsheets.

1

Availability submission

Guards report weekly availability in a Telegram Web App — no extra app, no passwords.

2

Constraints workbook

A styled, color-coded RTL Excel export of every submission — round-trip importable.

3

Scheduling board

Availability paints the board; smart manual assignment with live soft warnings.

4

Publish & lock

Each guard gets a personal message + a PNG of the full schedule; locking is automatic.

5

Actual schedule

An editable copy of the plan for the running week — last-minute swaps, forever.

6

Attendance & comparison

Telegram punch clock, cross-checked minute-by-minute against the actual schedule.

7

Payroll workbooks

Payroll-bureau-ready Excel reports: standard vs. actual hours, fully transparent.

The outcome: an accurate, attendance-based payroll report — generated from real punches reconciled against what was actually scheduled, not what someone remembers.

Domain modeling

The week lifecycle — a three-state machine

Deliberately simple: no reopening, at most one open week, and one irreversible final state. Enforced twice — in service logic and in the database itself.

📢 "Publish" is a pure broadcast

It sends every guard a personal schedule + a PNG of the full board and stamps published_at — but never locks. Edit → republish, in a loop, until the week begins.

🔒 Locking only via rollover

LOCKED happens exactly when the start date arrives — automatically and silently, on Saturday night. It locks both the board and the constraints, for everyone.

1️⃣ One OPEN week, guaranteed

Opening is allowed only for the upcoming, never-yet-opened week. Enforced by service logic and a partial unique index in PostgreSQL — a race cannot break it.

🔁 Automation + self-healing

Opening and locking are scheduled from the settings page; a startup catch-up covers any missed midnight. Retention keeps the last 60 weeks, automatically.

Feature highlights

Four subsystems, one philosophy

"The system surfaces, the human decides" — soft warnings everywhere, hard blocks almost nowhere.

🏗️

Schedule Builder

  • Activation profiles (routine / holiday / event) — reusable position sets, assignable per week.
  • A position = a demand for one guard: days, hours and required attributes (armed, shift commander, patrol car).
  • Selecting a guard paints the board by their real submitted availability — full or partial coverage.
  • Click or drag to assign; up to two guards per cell with a draggable hand-off divider (30-minute snaps).
  • 9 live soft warnings — out of availability, double booking, missing attribute, under-rest, over-hours… flagging, never blocking.
  • Background auto-save with debounce — no save button, no lost work.
🛠️

Actual Schedule Layer

  • Born automatically the moment a week starts — a 1:1 private copy of the plan (idempotent lazy seeding covers history and downtime).
  • Editable forever — including finished weeks, for retroactive payroll fixes. No time gate, no availability checks.
  • Ad-hoc positions for unexpected events, tagged "added"; external reinforcement guards with their own report, kept out of payroll.
  • "Save as profile" — a good week becomes a reusable template.
  • A field-by-field identity test proves a fresh copy reads exactly like the plan.
🕐

Attendance Engine

  • Two-tap punch clock in Telegram — persistent 🟢/🔴 keyboard, native location sharing, GPS geofence that marks but doesn't block.
  • Raw events are append-only — the repository refuses updates and deletes; double-taps deduplicated.
  • Idempotent pairing builds actual shifts: cross-midnight nights, orphan punches, a 16-hour cap.
  • A comparison engine classifies every minute: covered / small gap / large gap / no-show / beyond plan / future.
  • Admin edits are audited append-only with a mandatory reason; three automatic Telegram alerts (no-show, 12h+ shift, <8h rest).
📊

Payroll Reports

  • A row for every calendar day: standard from the actual schedule, actual from the punches, signed surplus/deficit computed automatically.
  • Two RTL Excel workbooks faithful to the payroll bureau's own sample — per-employee (with totals and signature blocks) and a center-wide summary.
  • Full transparency: admin-edited punches highlighted in orange; rounded checkout shown beside the raw time; a missing checkout counts 0 hours until fixed — no phantom hours.
  • One-click download from the month view.
